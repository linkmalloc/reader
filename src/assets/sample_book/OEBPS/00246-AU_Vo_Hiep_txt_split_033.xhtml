The Road to Freedom by Hiep Vo is a true story. The author delved deeply into details of the complex-dangerous situation during his preparation for the outgoing of freedom.

The story is very strange to everybody, especially to the ones who have not lost freedom. The readers would ask themselves, “Is it a real-life story?”

The Road to Freedom is too hard and risky. The dear price to pay for freedom is the life of the author and all the people who want to get there. The story is very attractive and amazes the reader from beginning to end.

—Kevin (Khoa) Dinh
Author’s cousin
Former Captain of ARVN

The number of persons actually executed by communist cadre carrying out the land reform program has been variously estimated. Some estimates of those killed range up to 200,000. Author Bernard Fall estimated executions at 50,000. Other scholarship has concluded that the higher estimates were based on political propaganda emanating from South Vietnam and that the actual total of those executed was probably much lower. Moise’s conclusions were supported by documents of Hungarian diplomats living in North Vietnam at the time of the land reform.
