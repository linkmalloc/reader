However, the problem has two cases: the first case is αo = 0o and the second one is αo ≠ 0. The first case is relatively easy, but the second one is hard. I thought for graduation, the teacher should assign the second one.

The exam day came. I went to the exam center, lovesick.

When the teacher handed out the problem, I saw the problem was the electromagnetic wave enters a brass rod with α· = 0. However, in my mind, I still saw α· ≠ 0. I smiled and talked to myself, “Exactly as I predicted.”

The result, I had to solve the tougher problem but I was wrong, then I failed.

In that summer, one more time, I used lessons as the weapon to fight the lovesickness.

Three months later, I took the exam again. This time, the lovesickness was reduced, then I got the first place easily.

The graduation exam for Faculty of Pedagogy include subjects from the Faculty of Sciences such as math and physics. Moreover, a student had to take exam for education and teaching practice. Students failed because subjects belonging to the Faculty of Sciences, which are very hard, were taught in French. The subjects for education and teaching were so easy; no one failed those so far.

The practical math teaching exam was conducted by an old teacher. And the old one was often influenced by Confucianism, then they were so severe. According to Confucianism, our people have a proverb: Quân, Sư, Phụ (君 師 父). It means in society, the most respected person is the king or emperor, the second is the teacher, and the third one is the father. So everyone looked up to the teacher a lot.

To me, it is not valid for our generation for these reasons:

 

  1. In the old time, the teachers were as old as the student’s father. The teachers usually were retired mandarin. But for new teachers of our age, we were trained at very young age. We were only a couple years older than our students, especially for senior high.
  2. For our generation, a teacher may go to other schools to learn more. Unluckily, the instructor may be the former student. Some teachers were drafted to attend military school. Instructors usually were the former students.

 

So who is the real teacher?

On the day I took teaching practice, I already took out a white shirt, cleaned, then hanged it at the yard to dry. In early morning, I ironed, then put it on with a tie. Unfortunately, that morning, it showered heavily, so my shirt was wet. Our country was an undeveloped one; majority of us were poor. My family had no exception. I had to contribute my scholarship to my mom in order to improve family conditions so that I had only one good white shirt for special case or teaching practice. So I thought to take an old military khaki shirt without tie. That one I usually had on for schooling because I could buy it very cheap and it was durable.

Another unlucky thing was I practiced teaching at Trung Vuong High, a female student school. My habit is always making others happy by laughing or smiling. So at school, students always laugh while learning.

When my practicing over, my old teacher pointed to me and said: “You see, all of your friends have white shirts with tie. Only you have khaki shirt, no tie. While teaching, you make female students laugh. You do not deserve to be a teacher. I would fail you.”

On the day the result was posted, my name was not on the list.

The university committee summoned a meeting and made another decision. The new list was posted, and my name was in the last place.

By that time, I had graduated from the university, but I did not know where I should teach. I went to the Department of Education to get the advice of Mr. Pham Dang Canh, my former teacher at Vung Tau High. Mr. Canh, at that time, was the director of the high school section of the Education Department.

He was surprised to see me.

After some formal conversation, he asked, “Hiep, do you have any trouble that you come here to look for me?”

I said, “Teacher, I graduated from the University of Pedagogy, but I don’t know where I should teach.”

“What grade did you graduate?”

“The last place.”

“The last place? Why did you study that badly?”

I said, “Actually, I’ve learned mathematics and physics pretty well, but I failed in teaching practice.”

He frowned. “If you failed in teaching practice, you cannot graduate. I am really confused. Can you make it clear?”

I clarified, “Teacher, I passed mathematics and physics with a very high score. But on the day we went to practice at Trung Vuong Girl School, I failed.”

“Why’s that?”

“On that day, I did not have a white shirt and tie like the other students, and when teaching, I sometimes made the students laugh. The teacher who takes care of us is a very old person, so his point of view in teaching differs from mine. He thinks a teacher should have a tie and have a serious manner when teaching, so he failed me.”

“But why did you graduate?”

“Because my physics score was very high, the University Committee discussed that matter and allowed me to graduate but to select the school for teaching, as I was in last place.”

Mr. Canh gave a nod of assent: “Yes, math subject is very hard to study. Every year, only a dozen can graduate, but more than forty schools need math teachers. Moreover, we spent a lot of money for you guys.”

“Thank you, Teacher, for your understanding.”

“Do you have the list of open jobs?”

“I don’t, but I remember all places have been selected.”

Mr. Canh went to a cabinet, took out a list, and came back.

“Now, you cross out all the selected places for me,” he said.

He handed me the list; I crossed off those locations and returned it to him.

He asked, “You teach math at private school too, right?”

“Yes, sir,” I answered.

“When I taught at Thoi Dai Private School, I met you. Do you still want to teach private school in Saigon again?”

“Yes, sir!”

He looked at the list and said, “So you should go to Cho Lach High. Cho Lach is a small district of Vinh Long Province, eighty kilometers southwest of Saigon. However, you can return to Saigon to teach at some centers, which train students in mathematics and physics for baccalaureates.”

“Thank you, Teacher.”

Then I went home.

I thought, Cho Lach33 is not a good name at all. It seems to be a very rural countryside; perhaps there is a market that is surrounded by reeds. Should I select that location? Mr. Canh did inform me about it, so in any case, I had to go there.

That evening, I went to my martial arts class to bid farewell to my friends.

Sanh, a friend of mine, asked, “Where will you teach, Hiep?”

“Cho Lach High.”

Dat, Sanh’s brother, said, “Really? That is our native village.”

I asked, “How is it?”

Phat, another of Sanh’s brothers, replied, “It’s very boring but a peaceful countryside.”
